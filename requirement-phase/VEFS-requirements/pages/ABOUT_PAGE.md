# VEFS About Us Page Requirements

**Status:** In Progress
**Priority:** High (Second Priority)
**Created:** 2025-12-23

## Page Overview

The About Us page establishes trust and credibility by telling VEFS's story, clarifying the foundation's mission and vision, showcasing core values, highlighting achievements, and introducing the leadership team (Founder and Trustees). This page should inspire visitors and create an emotional connection to the organization's purpose.

## Page Structure & Sections

### 1. Page Header
- **Status:** Required
- **Content:**
  - Page title: "About Us" or "About VEFS"
  - Hero image or background (nature-themed, representing VEFS's work)
  - Subheading: Brief tagline (e.g., "Restoring Ecology, Empowering Communities")
- **Design Notes:**
  - Consistent with home page header styling
  - Full-width banner with image
  - Text overlay for readability

---

### 2. Foundation Story / Introduction
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** Narrative text section
- **Content:**
  - 2-3 paragraph introduction explaining:
    - How VEFS was founded and why
    - The inspiration behind the foundation
    - Current scope and reach
    - Connection to ancient Tamil literature and indigenous trees
- **Design Notes:**
  - Engaging, accessible language
  - Emotional yet professional tone
  - Include historical context of Sangha Ilakiya
  - Optional: Pull-quotes or highlighted passages

---

### 3. Mission & Vision Statements
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** Two prominent sections or side-by-side blocks
- **Content:**

#### Mission
- Clear, concise statement describing VEFS's purpose
- How the foundation serves its communities
- Focus on action and impact

#### Vision
- Long-term aspiration for ecological and social change
- What the world will look like if VEFS succeeds
- Inspirational and forward-looking

**Design Notes:**
- Large, readable typography
- Distinct visual treatment (background color, icon, or styling)
- Consider using quotation marks or special formatting
- Optional: Include founding date and location

---

### 4. Core Values & Guiding Principles
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** 4-6 value cards or visual blocks
- **Content:**
  Each value should include:
  - Value name (e.g., "Sustainability," "Community," "Education")
  - Description (2-3 sentences explaining the principle)
  - Optional: Icon representing the value

**Suggested Values for VEFS:**
- **Ecological Restoration:** Commitment to planting and preserving indigenous trees
- **Indigenous Knowledge:** Honoring ancient Tamil wisdom and traditional practices
- **Education & Awareness:** Empowering people through knowledge and experiential learning
- **Community Empowerment:** Supporting farmers, women, students, and local communities
- **Sustainability:** Creating long-term environmental and social impact
- **Inclusivity:** Engaging all segments of society in our mission

**Design Notes:**
- Visual grid layout (2-3 columns on desktop, 1 on mobile)
- Icons or colors to distinguish each value
- Easy to scan and understand

---

### 5. Impact & Achievements
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** Key metrics and statistics displayed visually
- **Content:**
  Display quantifiable achievements such as:
  - Number of trees planted
  - Beneficiaries reached (students, farmers, women, public)
  - Workshops/awareness sessions conducted
  - Geographic coverage (villages, districts, regions)
  - Years of operation
  - Volunteer/staff members
  - Partnerships formed

**Design Notes:**
- Use visually striking numbers (large fonts, icons)
- Cards or boxes for each metric
- Consider infographic-style presentation
- Optional: Icons or illustrations representing each metric
- Include time period for statistics (e.g., "Since 2015" or "In 2024")

---

### 6. Journey / Timeline of Growth
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** Visual timeline or chronological narrative
- **Content:**
  Key milestones in VEFS's history:
  - Foundation year and founding story
  - First major initiative or milestone
  - Expansion of programs
  - Key partnerships formed
  - Significant achievements or awards
  - Current status and future outlook

**Design Notes:**
- Timeline format (vertical or horizontal)
- Year labels and milestone descriptions
- Optional: Photos or icons for each milestone
- Narrate the growth journey
- End with vision for the future

---

### 7. Founder Profile
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** Dedicated section with prominent styling
- **Content:**
  - Founder's name and title
  - Professional photo (high quality)
  - Brief bio (3-4 paragraphs):
    - Background and education
    - What inspired them to found VEFS
    - Personal mission and values
    - Why they chose to focus on indigenous trees and ecology
  - Optional: Founder's quote or message
  - Optional: Contact or social media links

**Design Notes:**
- High-quality, professional photograph
- Larger text size than other sections
- Personal, warm tone
- Consider sidebar layout (photo on left, text on right, or vice versa)
- Optional: Highlighted quote from founder

---

### 8. Trustees / Board of Directors
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** List with minimal information
- **Content:**
  For each Trustee:
  - Name
  - Title/Position on board
  - Brief professional background (1-2 sentences)
  - Optional: Small profile photo

**Design Notes:**
- Table or list format (not elaborate cards)
- Simple, scannable layout
- Less emphasis than founder profile
- Optional: Click-to-expand for more detailed bios
- Show diversity of board expertise

**Note:** More detailed individual trustee profiles could be available on separate pages if needed.

---

- Highlight diversity and expertise

### 11. Recognition & Awards

### 12. Call-to-Action Section
- **Status:** Required
- **Format:** Multiple pathways to engagement
- **Content:**
  - "Join Our Movement" CTA
  - Options presented:
    - Become a Volunteer
    - Donate to VEFS
    - Attend an Event
    - Join a Training/Workshop

**Design Notes:**
- End of About page
- Encourage visitor to take next step
- Multiple options cater to different interests
- Visually prominent

## Design & Visual Approach

### Layout
- **Desktop:** Multi-column layouts with balanced whitespace
- **Tablet:** 2-column grids, adjusted spacing
- **Mobile:** Single-column stack with optimized touch targets
- **Consistency:** Match home page design system

### Color & Typography
- **Primary Colors:** Green (eco-focus) + professional secondary
- **Typography:** Same fonts as home page
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Clear headings, readable body text
- **Accent Elements:** Icons, images, and quotes to break up text

### Visual Elements
- **Founder Photo:** Professional, high-quality portrait
- **Timeline Graphics:** Clear, visually engaging
- **Icons:** Represent values and achievements
- **Images:** Showcase VEFS's work and impact
- **Whitespace:** Generous padding for readability

---

## Responsive Design

### Desktop (1024px+)
- Full-width sections with sidebars
- Multi-column grids
- Large images and typography

### Tablet (768px - 1023px)
- 2-column layouts where applicable
- Adjusted image sizes
- Consolidated sidebars

### Mobile (< 768px)
- Single-column stack
- Full-width sections
- Touch-friendly buttons and links
- Hamburger navigation

## Interactions & Animations

- **Smooth scrolling** between sections
- **Fade-in animations** for content as page loads/scrolls
- **Hover effects** on buttons and interactive elements
- **Expandable sections** (optional) for trustee bios
- **Timeline animations** (optional) as user scrolls

## Accessibility

- **Alt text** for all images (photos, icons, graphics)
- **Semantic HTML** for screen readers
- **Color contrast** sufficient for readability (WCAG AA minimum)
- **Keyboard navigation** fully supported
- **ARIA labels** for complex sections
- **Readable fonts** and appropriate sizing

---

## SEO Considerations

- **Page Title:** Include "About" and "VEFS"
- **Meta Description:** Compelling 150-160 character summary
- **Keywords:** Foundation, ecological, indigenous trees, Tamil Nadu, social welfare, etc.
- **H1 Tag:** Clear main heading (usually "About Us")
- **Structured Data:** Organization schema, person schema for founder/trustees
- **Internal Links:** Links to relevant programs, events, and donation pages
